# SEO action list for Aviara Design Co.

**Goal:** show up when people Google "staging temecula," "home staging temecula," "staging companies near me," and the same queries for every other Southern California city Aviara serves.

The website side of the puzzle has been built: dedicated city landing pages, structured data (LocalBusiness + FAQ schema), the sitemap, redirects from the old WordPress URLs, and 1000+ word locally-tailored content for Temecula, Murrieta, Menifee, and Fallbrook. That's the on-page foundation.

The items below are the **off-page** half — the parts that live in Google Business Profile, third-party directories, and review platforms. None of these require touching the website. All of them move the needle for local search rankings, often more than the on-page work does.

Tackle them in the order below. Items 1 and 2 alone can lift rankings noticeably within a few weeks.

---

## 1. Google Business Profile — fix two things, optimize the rest

You already have a GBP set up (Aviara Design Co., 5.0 stars, 11 reviews, Home staging service). That's a great starting point. Two things to fix and a handful of optimizations to add.

### A. Fix the hours discrepancy

The website currently says "Mon–Fri: By appointment / Sat: By appointment / Sun: Closed." Google Business Profile shows "Opens 9 AM Mon" with implied real hours.

Google cross-references your website and your GBP for consistency. When they don't match, it weakens your local ranking.

**Decide what the real public-facing hours are**, then make both match exactly. For a service business like Aviara, showing real hours (e.g., "9 AM – 5 PM, Mon–Fri / 9 AM – 3 PM Sat / Closed Sun") looks more legitimate to Google than "By appointment" (which Google's algorithm treats as ambiguous).

Once you decide, send the hours back so they can be updated on the website to match.

### B. Add secondary business categories

In GBP, you can have up to **9 secondary categories** in addition to your primary one. Right now Aviara only has the primary ("Home staging service"). That means you're not surfacing for related searches.

**Steps:** GBP dashboard → Edit profile → Business information → Category → Add secondary category. Add all of these:

- **Interior designer**
- **Furniture rental service** (vacant staging fits this)
- **Real estate consultant** (staging consultations fit this)

Each category adds query types that can bring you to the top of the local pack.

### C. Add more photos

Listings with 20+ photos rank visibly higher in the local pack. Aim for **30+ photos** total over the next 30 days. A mix of:

- 10–15 staged room shots (the listing photos from past projects — coordinate with the agents/photographers)
- 5–8 detail shots (vignettes, styled accessories, the kind of close-ups that show your aesthetic)
- 3–5 "before and after" pairs if you have them
- A few photos of you, Dad, and Mom on a job site (faces build trust)

Upload in GBP → Photos → Add Photo. Don't dump them all in one day — drip a few each week so Google sees ongoing activity.

### D. Fill in the Services list

GBP has a "Services" section that most businesses leave blank. Filling it in helps you surface for "vacant staging" / "occupied staging" / etc. queries directly.

**Steps:** GBP dashboard → Edit profile → Services → Add service.

Add each of these with a short description and price range (if you're comfortable showing them — even a "From $X" rough range helps):

- **Vacant Home Staging** — "Full furniture inventory delivery, 30-day rentals, listing-day photography ready."
- **Occupied Home Staging** — "On-site styling, furniture editing & layout, accessory & art layering."
- **Interior Design** — "Concept & material palette, custom furnishings, procurement & install."
- **Staging Consultations** — "90-minute walkthrough, room-by-room playbook, trade & vendor referrals."

### E. Explicitly list Service Areas

GBP lets you list specific cities as places you serve. This explicitly tells Google "rank us for these locations."

**Steps:** GBP dashboard → Edit profile → Location and areas → Service area → Add areas.

Add: **Temecula, Murrieta, Menifee, Fallbrook, San Diego, Carlsbad, Oceanside, Encinitas, Vista, Escondido, Irvine, Newport Beach, Laguna Beach, Mission Viejo, Lake Forest, Riverside, Corona, Lake Elsinore, Wildomar, Hemet, Perris, San Marcos** — and any other cities you actually take projects in. Adding more cities than you serve is risky (Google can flag it), so be honest about your service area.

### F. Pre-populate the Q&A section

The GBP Q&A is **public** — strangers can ask questions, and anyone can answer them (including competitors). If you don't put your own questions there first, you can end up with random or unhelpful answers up there for weeks.

**Steps:** GBP listing (in Google Maps view) → "Questions & answers" → Ask a question (you can do this from your own account).

Post 4–5 of your own questions and immediately answer them as the business. Use these (they're the same ones already on the city pages):

1. "How much does home staging cost in Temecula?"
2. "How quickly can you stage a listing?"
3. "Do you stage occupied homes (when the family is still living there)?"
4. "Do you serve all of Southern California?"
5. "Is staging worth it for newer/lower-priced homes?"

### G. Post Google Posts weekly

GBP has a "Posts" feature that works like a mini social feed on your listing. Posts older than 7 days lose visibility — so consistency matters more than length.

**Steps:** GBP dashboard → Add update.

Post 1–2 times per week. What to post:

- Recent project (1–2 photos + 1–2 sentences)
- A tip ("Three things to do before staging photos")
- An offer or seasonal note ("Spring listing prep — book by [date]")
- Anything new on the website (new city page, new blog post)

Each post is another freshness signal Google likes.

## 2. Reviews — get to 25+

You're at 11 reviews / 5.0 average — already a great rating, but ranking jumps meaningfully when you cross **25 reviews**. That's the next target.

**Steps:**

1. **Make a list** of every client from the last 12 months whose project went well (sellers, agents). Aim for 15–20 names.
2. **Get your "review link"** from GBP: dashboard → Home → Get more reviews → Copy the link. It's a short URL that takes them directly to the review form.
3. **Send each one a short message** — text or email, whichever you have. Keep it to two sentences. Something like:

   > Hi [name] — Brooklyn here from Aviara. So glad we got to work on [property/project]. If you'd be willing to leave a quick Google review, the link is here: [link]. Thank you!

4. **Spread them out** — sending 15 review requests in one day produces 15 reviews in one day, which Google's spam filter sometimes flags. Send 2–3 per week over a month or two.
5. **Reply to every review you get** — even short ones. "Thanks, [name]! Loved working on the [property]." Replies show Google the business is active and engaged, and they boost the review's weight in ranking.
6. **Ask agents specifically** — agents who refer Aviara consistently are gold. Get reviews from them mentioning specific city names ("Brooklyn staged my Temecula listing in two weeks…"). Those city mentions inside review text directly help that city's rankings.

## 3. Get listed on industry directories

Each directory listing does two things: gives Google another "this business exists at this address/phone" signal (called a "citation"), and produces a backlink to the website. Both help rankings.

**Critical rule:** the business **Name, Address, and Phone (NAP)** must be **identical** everywhere — every directory, every social profile, the website, GBP. Differences (e.g., "Aviara Design Co." vs. "Aviara Design Co LLC", or "(949) 697-1618" vs. "949.697.1618") get flagged by Google as inconsistency and weaken rankings.

**Use this exact format everywhere:**

- Name: **Aviara Design Co.**
- Phone: **(949) 697-1618**
- Website: **https://www.aviaradesignco.com**
- Email: **aviaradesignco@gmail.com**

### Sign up on these directories (do these first):

These are the highest-impact ones for home staging:

1. **Yelp** — https://biz.yelp.com — claim the listing if it exists, create it if not. Add 5+ photos.
2. **Houzz** — https://www.houzz.com/pro/signup — extremely important for home staging/interior design. Add a portfolio.
3. **Angi** (formerly Angie's List) — https://www.angi.com/business
4. **BBB (Better Business Bureau)** — https://www.bbb.org/get-listed — accreditation costs money but a free listing is free.
5. **Thumbtack** — https://www.thumbtack.com/pro — works well for staging consultations specifically.
6. **HomeAdvisor** — https://www.homeadvisor.com/pro
7. **Bing Places for Business** — https://www.bingplaces.com — separate from Google, ~5% of US searches.
8. **Apple Maps Connect** — https://mapsconnect.apple.com — Apple users searching in Apple Maps for "staging near me" find businesses listed here.

### Local Chamber of Commerce listings:

Membership is sometimes paid, but a basic directory listing is often free or very cheap. The backlink from a `.org` chamber site is worth more than 50 generic directory listings.

- Temecula Valley Chamber of Commerce — https://www.temecula.org
- Murrieta Chamber of Commerce — https://murrietachamber.org
- Menifee Valley Chamber of Commerce — https://menifeevalleychamber.com
- Fallbrook Chamber of Commerce — https://www.fallbrookchamberofcommerce.org

Sign up for at least the Temecula one (closest to home base). If budget allows, the others too.

### Industry-specific:

- **RESA (Real Estate Staging Association)** — https://realestatestagingassociation.com — the industry trade association. Membership has perks and a directory listing.
- **StagedHomes.com** — directory for ASP-certified stagers (if applicable).

## 4. Local backlinks — quality over quantity

A single backlink from a real local website (a Temecula Realtor's blog, a local home magazine, a local newspaper) is worth more for SEO than 100 directory listings combined.

Ways to get them:

1. **Partner with local Realtors.** Offer to write a guest post for their blog — "5 things to do before listing your Temecula home." They get free content, you get a local backlink.
2. **Pitch local press.** Temecula Valley News, Murrieta Patch, North County Times — any local publication might run a profile or guest article. Pitch a story angle: "Family-owned staging studio in Temecula sees 25%+ faster sales for staged listings." Reporters love specific numbers.
3. **Sponsor or speak at a local event.** Open Houses, Realtor meetings, home tours — these often produce mention/backlinks in event recaps.
4. **Be a vendor at a Realtor association event.** Greater Temecula Valley Association of Realtors, North San Diego County Association of Realtors. Vendor listings often include backlinks.
5. **Send before/after photos to local lifestyle publications.** Even a small mention in a "local businesses to know" roundup gets you a backlink.

This is the slowest of the items but compounds the longest. One real local backlink a month for a year = 12 quality backlinks = significant ranking lift.

- **Post a new Google Post** weekly (project, tip, or seasonal note).
- **Ask 1–2 recent clients for reviews** weekly until you hit 50.
- **Check the Q&A section on GBP** for any new questions from strangers; answer promptly.
